2 Hero Slider — Separate Desktop & Mobile Images

These are the BIG images that customers see first. You upload 2 different images per slide — one for desktop (landscape) and one for mobile (portrait). The website automatically shows the right one.

💡 Why 2 images per slide?
Desktop screens are wide (landscape). Mobile screens are tall (portrait).
A landscape photo gets cropped badly on mobile. So you shoot TWO versions:

Desktop: Wide shot — model on left, space for text on right
Mobile: Vertical shot — model centered, close-up, text overlays on top
SlideDesktop Image (1600×900)Mobile Image (600×900)Text Overlay
Slide 1 Wide shot: model in suit on LEFT side, empty space on right for text. Landscape. Close-up: same model, portrait crop. Model fills the frame vertically. "Tradition Meets Elegance"
Slide 2 Wide shot: embroidery/stitching scene. Hands working, fabric spread wide. Vertical: close-up of hands stitching, or fabric detail portrait shot. "Your Design, Our Craft"
Slide 3 Wide shot: juttis arranged in a horizontal row on marble counter. Vertical: 2-3 juttis stacked vertically, close-up embroidery. "Punjabi Jutti Collection"
Slide 4 Wide shot: full boutique interior panorama. Vertical: boutique corner view — counter + seating, portrait crop. "Visit Our Boutique"

How to Shoot Desktop vs Mobile:

🖥️ Desktop Image

1600 × 900 Landscape

Hold phone sideways (horizontal). Keep subject on one side — leave empty space for text overlay on the other side. Wide, cinematic feel.

📱 Mobile Image

600 × 900 Portrait

Hold phone upright (vertical). Subject centered, fills the frame. Close-up works great. Text overlays on top/bottom.

📸 Pro tip: For each slide, take BOTH versions at the same location:
1. First shoot landscape (phone sideways) → Desktop image
2. Then shoot portrait (phone upright) → Mobile image
3. Same subject, same lighting, just different orientation

You can add unlimited slides! 4 is recommended, but you can have 6, 8, or more.
⚠️ Do NOT use AI-generated images for hero slides. Customers can tell immediately. Real photos of your real products in your real boutique build trust.

How to Upload in Admin:

Admin → Homepage → Hero Slider tab → Each slide has TWO upload buttons:

  • 🖥️ Desktop Image — upload landscape photo
  • 📱 Mobile Image — upload portrait photo
  • Website automatically shows the right one based on screen size
  • Click "+ Add Slide" to add more slides (unlimited)
  • Click trash icon to remove a slide

8 Photography Tips — How to Shoot

📱 Phone Settings

  • Use highest resolution (iPhone: 12MP, Samsung: 50MP/108MP)
  • Turn OFF flash — use natural light or your track lighting
  • Turn ON grid lines (helps with alignment)
  • For products: use Portrait mode (blurs background)
  • For boutique shots: use Wide angle
  • Hold phone steady — lean against wall if needed

💡 Lighting

  • Your boutique has excellent track lighting — USE IT
  • Shoot during daytime for natural light + track lights together
  • Avoid direct sunlight (creates harsh shadows)
  • For fabric close-ups: take near window with soft light
  • For juttis on marble: your counter area has perfect lighting

🎯 Backgrounds

  • Suits on mannequin: Place against the white wall in your shop
  • Suits on model: Your seating area (chairs, jute rug) makes a great backdrop
  • Juttis/footwear: On your marble counter — looks premium
  • Fabric flat lay: On the wooden table or marble surface
  • Keep backgrounds clean and uncluttered

📝 Editing (Free Apps)

  • Snapseed (Google, free) — brightness, warmth, crop, sharpen
  • Canva (free) — add text to banner images, resize images
  • Remove.bg (website, free) — remove background from product photos
  • Squoosh.app (website, free) — compress images to smaller file size
